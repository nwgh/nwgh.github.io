I am writing as a concerned citizen and person of faith in opposition to the proposed change in public charge rules.

The United States has for over a hundred years been identified with the Statue of Liberty. This statue bears upon its pedestal a sonnet containing the phrase “Give me your tired, your poor, your huddled masses yearning to breathe free” — a phrase that has also come to be identified with immigration in the United States. And indeed, this has historically been the aspiration of immigration in the United States — to be a beacon of hope for persons whose home countries will not take care of them. This proposed change fundamentally undermines our aspirations as a nation.

In the United States, much is often made about our society’s roots in the Judeo-Christian heritage. As a Christian, I am called to welcome the stranger. The Hebrew Bible is filled with admonitions to care for the foreigner sojourning in your lands, as is the New Testament. I must stand with my brothers and sisters who have been endowed by their Creator with the same inalienable rights I have. For the United States to attempt to shirk its duties of care for foreigners is nothing less than a rebuke of the Judeo-Christian heritage that we claim to be so proud of.

We can not allow our country to simultaneously abandon our heritage and our hopes for the future. It is for these reasons, and for the sake of our nation’s integrity, that I oppose the Department of Homeland Security’s proposed changes to the public charge rule.
